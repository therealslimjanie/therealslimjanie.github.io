Blog Post Two

Why I Deleted My Facebook...And Why You Should as Well.

October 11, 2019

If you have never heard of Facebook, then either you are above the age of ninety years old or you truly live under a rock. A week and a half ago, I deleted my Facebook. To be completely honest, I have been wanting to delete my Facebook for some time now. I have been holding back though because Facebook can be seen as an incredible form of communication for me and my 20-something friends. More importantly, it allowed me to buy vintage sorority clothes (which was really the only thing keeping me there). Facebook has been around for thirteen years now and it has developed into a massive media conglomerate. Facebook is not only a social media site; it is a marketplace, video creation platform, and a news source. This media boom has allowed for Facebook to become the superior social media platform. But appearances can deceive, and no platform is more deceptive than Facebook.

Facebook is threat to personal security, due to their policies on AI, as well as their predatory nature when it comes to ads. Now, you must be wondering where did all of this start and why is looking at funny memes of cats going to be a threat to my human security? Well, neither of these questions are easy to answer. Hopefully though, the next couple of paragraphs will help you to better understand and hopefully delete your Facebook.
